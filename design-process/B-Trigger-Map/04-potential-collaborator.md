# Potential Collaborator - Secondary Visitor Mode

> Secondary mode: authentic overlap and low-pressure contact.

**Priority:** Secondary
**Role in Strategy:** Ensures emergent opportunities can happen without designing for conversion.
**Created:** 2026-04-21

---

## Profile Summary

The Potential Collaborator notices overlap in technical, creative, entrepreneurial, investment, or intellectual interests. They need evidence of process, taste, and direction before deciding whether contact would be meaningful.

---

## Current Situation

Collaborators often need more than a polished result. They need to understand how someone thinks, what they care about, and whether there is enough shared context to justify reaching out.

The Potential Collaborator needs:

- Process evidence.
- Domain overlap.
- Clear artifact context.
- A contact route that feels available but not needy.
- Signals of taste and judgment.

---

## Psychological Profile

**Core motivation:**

- Find genuine intersections.
- Evaluate compatibility.
- Understand working style indirectly.
- Avoid transactional outreach.

**Browsing style:**

- Moves through related work.
- Looks for decisions and constraints.
- Reads notes for worldview and taste.
- Checks timeline for consistency and evolution.

---

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers

**1. Evidence of process**

- Wants to see how things were made, not only what was produced.
- Success means artifact pages include context, decisions, and constraints.
- **Archive promise:** Project entries can show process and evolution.

**2. Cross-domain compatibility**

- Wants to know whether interests overlap across more than one dimension.
- Success means collections and tags reveal intersections.
- **Archive promise:** Multi-membership allows one artifact to show technical and creative relevance.

**3. Low-pressure contact**

- Wants to reach out only if the overlap is genuine.
- Success means contact is available but not framed as a conversion endpoint.
- **Archive promise:** Contact remains quiet, optional, and context-respecting.

### Negative Drivers

**1. Empty polish**

- Fears impressive presentation without substance.
- Failure looks like a glossy portfolio with little process.
- **Archive answer:** Emphasize artifacts, notes, and decision context.

**2. Unclear interests**

- Fears not knowing what kind of collaboration would make sense.
- Failure looks like broad categories without concrete examples.
- **Archive answer:** Tags, related artifacts, and Notes reveal patterns.

**3. Forced outreach**

- Fears contact language that feels transactional.
- Failure looks like "book a call" pressure.
- **Archive answer:** Use minimal contact language tied to genuine intersections.

---

## Transformation Journey

### Before the Archive

- The collaborator may know one artifact but not the broader context.
- Overlap is possible but hard to assess.
- Contact may feel premature.

### After the Archive

- The collaborator sees concrete work and process.
- Cross-domain patterns become visible.
- Contact feels justified only when there is a real reason.

---

## Design Requirements

- Artifact pages should include role, process, and related work.
- Tags should reveal intersections, not just categories.
- Notes should be readable as worldview evidence.
- Contact should be reachable from relevant contexts but visually restrained.
