# Professional Observer - Secondary Visitor Mode

> Secondary mode: competence inferred through artifacts and structure.

**Priority:** Secondary
**Role in Strategy:** Ensures professional readers can evaluate work without forcing professional signaling into the site.
**Created:** 2026-04-21

---

## Profile Summary

The Professional Observer may be evaluating credibility, capability, or fit. They are not the primary audience, but the site should still let them infer competence from concrete artifacts, breadth, and structure.

---

## Current Situation

Professional observers often expect conventional portfolio cues: roles, projects, outcomes, contact, and perhaps a resume. This project intentionally avoids performance language, but it still needs enough clarity to make evaluation possible.

The Professional Observer needs:

- Fast signal.
- Relevant examples.
- Context around role and contribution.
- Clear navigation by domain.
- Minimal friction to contact if appropriate.

---

## Psychological Profile

**Core motivation:**

- Evaluate capability.
- Find relevant work quickly.
- Separate evidence from claims.
- Understand breadth without being overwhelmed.

**Browsing style:**

- Starts with Selected or a specific collection.
- Scans summaries and metadata.
- Looks for outcomes, process, and quality of thinking.
- May leave if signal is buried.

---

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers

**1. Concrete evidence**

- Wants examples that demonstrate capability.
- Success means projects include enough context to inspect.
- **Archive promise:** Artifact pages show work, role, process, and links.

**2. Fast relevance filtering**

- Wants to move directly to engineering, entrepreneurship, investing, or art.
- Success means collection routes are obvious.
- **Archive promise:** Collections provide domain-specific access without fixed identity claims.

**3. Trajectory signal**

- Wants to see development over time.
- Success means timeline makes progression visible.
- **Archive promise:** Chronology and milestone emphasis reveal evolution.

### Negative Drivers

**1. Inflated positioning**

- Fears claims unsupported by work.
- Failure looks like grand language without evidence.
- **Archive answer:** Use restrained copy and artifact-first presentation.

**2. Weak signal hidden in clutter**

- Fears too much undifferentiated content.
- Failure looks like a messy archive with no curated path.
- **Archive answer:** Selected provides a compact entry layer.

**3. Unclear inspection path**

- Fears not finding the relevant work quickly.
- Failure looks like beautiful pages with poor retrieval.
- **Archive answer:** Use collections, tags, timeline, and metadata consistently.

---

## Transformation Journey

### Before the Archive

- The observer may only see scattered signals.
- It is hard to infer competence without a structured path.
- Breadth may look unfocused.

### After the Archive

- The observer can inspect relevant artifacts.
- Breadth reads as intentional multi-domain output.
- Competence emerges from evidence and trajectory.

---

## Design Requirements

- Selected should include representative breadth, not only conventional achievements.
- Collection pages must support quick scanning.
- Artifact pages must clarify role and context.
- Timeline should highlight milestones without overclaiming.
- About should be minimal but sufficient.
