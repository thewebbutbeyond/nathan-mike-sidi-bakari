# Curious Observer - Secondary Visitor Mode

> Secondary mode: non-linear exploration and sense-making.

**Priority:** Secondary
**Role in Strategy:** Tests whether public access remains legible without becoming audience-optimized.
**Created:** 2026-04-21

---

## Profile Summary

The Curious Observer arrives without a specific need. They may know Nathan, find a shared link, land on a note, or stumble across a project. They are not looking to be converted. They want to understand what this place is and whether there is anything worth following.

---

## Current Situation

Most personal sites make visitors decode either a resume pitch or a chronological blog. This site needs to make its archive model clear quickly while preserving room for wandering.

The Curious Observer needs:

- Immediate orientation.
- Obvious entry points.
- Low-pressure browsing.
- Plain explanations of unfamiliar artifact types.
- Enough curation to avoid feeling lost.

---

## Psychological Profile

**Core motivation:**

- Curiosity.
- Pattern recognition.
- Discovery.
- Desire for authentic signal.

**Browsing style:**

- Samples before committing.
- Moves from one interesting artifact to another.
- Uses tags, dates, and selected entries as clues.
- Leaves quickly if the site feels like a pitch.

---

## Driving Forces

### Positive Drivers

**1. Quick orientation**

- Wants to understand the site's shape without reading a manifesto.
- Success means the first screen reveals routes into the archive.
- **Archive promise:** Home / Portal exposes Selected, Timeline, Collections, and Notes immediately.

**2. Non-linear discovery**

- Wants to browse by interest rather than follow a scripted path.
- Success means tags, related artifacts, and timeline links create optional routes.
- **Archive promise:** Every artifact connects to identity and time.

**3. Authentic context**

- Wants enough explanation to understand why an artifact matters.
- Success means project pages include process and context, not just output.
- **Archive promise:** Artifact entries include summaries, metadata, and related links.

### Negative Drivers

**1. Being pitched to**

- Fears the site becoming a hidden sales funnel.
- Failure looks like inflated headlines and oversized CTAs.
- **Archive answer:** Keep calls to action optional and low-emphasis.

**2. Vague personal branding**

- Fears generic language that says little.
- Failure looks like "passionate multidisciplinary creator" without evidence.
- **Archive answer:** Let artifact lists, notes, and timelines explain the person.

**3. No entry point**

- Fears an archive that is technically complete but hard to begin.
- Failure looks like a wall of undifferentiated content.
- **Archive answer:** Selected provides a small curated start without replacing exploration.

---

## Transformation Journey

### Before the Archive

- The visitor has curiosity but no map.
- Nathan's domains may seem unrelated.
- There is no obvious first route.

### After the Archive

- The visitor understands the system quickly.
- They choose a path based on interest.
- They leave with a clearer sense of breadth and trajectory.

---

## Design Requirements

- Home / Portal must communicate archive structure quickly.
- Selected must be small, useful, and non-performative.
- Artifact summaries must be scannable.
- Related links should support wandering.
- Notes should be followable but not pushed.
