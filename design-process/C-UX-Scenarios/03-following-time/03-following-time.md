# 03: Future Nathan's Timeline Recall

**Project:** Nathan Mike Sidi Bakari
**Created:** 2026-04-21
**Method:** Whiteport Design Studio (WDS)

---

## Transaction

**What this scenario covers:**
Future Nathan uses the timeline to recover what happened, identify a milestone, and reconnect it to related work.

---

## Business Goal

**Goal:** Preserve continuity.
**Objective:** Artifacts remain retrievable and understandable over time through chronology and metadata.

---

## User & Situation

**Visitor Mode:** Future Nathan (Primary)
**Situation:** Months or years later, Nathan wants to remember when a piece of work happened and how it connected to other output.

---

## Mental State

**Trigger:** He remembers part of an artifact or period but not enough context.

**Hope:** The timeline can recover the artifact, context, and adjacent work.

**Worry:** Work has scattered into fragments that cannot be reassembled.

---

## Device & Starting Point

**Device:** Desktop.
**Entry:** He opens the site directly and chooses Timeline from the Home / Portal.

---

## Best Outcome

**User Success:**
Nathan finds the period, opens the artifact, and recovers the context and related links.

**Archive Success:**
Timeline proves the archive can preserve continuity and trajectory.

---

## Shortest Path

1. **Home / Portal** - Nathan chooses Timeline.
2. **Timeline** - Nathan scans dates, artifact types, and milestone emphasis.
3. **Artifact Detail** - Nathan opens the relevant artifact and reads context.
4. **Related Artifacts** - Nathan follows adjacent or related work to reconstruct the thread. ✓

---

## Trigger Map Connections

**Visitor Mode:** Future Nathan (Primary)

**Driving Forces Addressed:**

- ✅ **Want:** Durable recall.
- ✅ **Want:** Visible trajectory.
- ❌ **Fear:** Scattered memory.
- ❌ **Fear:** Maintenance entropy.

**Archive Goal:** Preserve continuity.

## Phase 4 Notes

Timeline is core infrastructure. It needs dates, artifact type, tags, summaries, and milestone emphasis without forcing a heroic narrative.
